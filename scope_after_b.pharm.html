SCOPE AFTER B.PHARM

After earning the B.Pharm degree, candidates can opt for a wide range of professional or study paths. Some of the options those bachelors of pharmacy candidates usually choose from include:

HIGHER EDUCATION OPTIONS:

Students can take up courses like M.Pharmacy, Postgraduate Diploma in Pharmacy, MBA in Pharmaceutical Management or even PhD to enhance their professional prospects in the long run.

DRUG INSPECTOR:

The role of a drug inspector comes into play when medications are authorized for sale amongst the general public. They are in charge of the quality of the medicinal products, thus ensuring that everything is manufactured and packaged as per the guidelines and protocols. Performing routine inspections is an essential aspect of this job. Vacancies for drug inspectors are available both in the central and state governments, making it a lucrative position.

R&D (RESEARCH & DEVELOPMENT) EXECUTIVE:

Formulation Development Associate is one of the key professionals responsible for the development of new products, drug formulations and processes systems. These people are experts in performing every aspect of drug development from scratch to manufacturing within the given deadlines. They also help in analyzing complex problems and providing solutions by conducting extensive lab experiments and assisting in report preparation. Additionally, they offer support to the technical team whenever needed.

CLINICAL RESEARCHER:

The role of a clinical researcher is particularly suited for individuals possessing a B.Pharm degree with a keen eye for research and development. They conduct comprehensive clinical trials and research studies for establishing which pharmaceutical products are safe to use. These professionals are also supposed to collaborate with doctors, consultants, investigators and regulatory bodies for performing the tests accurately. Apart from these, they also need to ensure the safety of the people involved in drug trials.

PHARMACY MANAGER:

Pharmacy managers are responsible for managing all the daily operations of a pharmacy such as dispensing prescription medicines, guiding customers on the usage of medications and arranging product displays among others. Not just that, they handle drug inventory alongside maintaining the record of orders and ensuring the safety of stored products. Being the in-charge of a pharmaceutical facility, their work also involves training and supervising the staff.

MEDICAL WRITER:

Medical writers are usually responsible for working with scientists, doctors and other subject matter experts for creating documents that clearly describe research results, product use and other important medical information. These professionals should have a keen interest in research as they are required to go through a lot of scientific research, medical journals, drug trials and clinical data to perform their jobs efficiently.

DRUG ANALYST:

Chemical technicians are professionals who hold a strong knowledge of medical equipment and laboratory testing. Their role is associated with collecting and delivering chemical samples to laboratories, assisting in lab testing and correctly documenting the lab testing reports. They also need to ensure that all the chemical processes are monitored appropriately. Safely storing tools, equipment and instruments and maintaining their proper function is an important part of their role as well.

PUBLIC SECTOR OPTIONS:

Working in the public sector allows candidates to not only earn decent salaries but also avail additional benefits like housing allowance, travel allowance and several retirement benefits. However, students interested in working in government organizations have to first pass competitive exams such as UPSC, NHM, ESIC, SBI, UPSEE, SSC, SGPGI and Railway Pharmacist, among others.

HOSPITAL PHARMACIST:

A hospital pharmacist is responsible for dispensing drugs and maintaining the pharmacy. Hospital pharmacists are in charge of controlling inventory and dispensing drugs in accordance with physician recommendations.

SALES AND MARKETING EXECUTIVE:

In the medical industry, sales and marketing jobs are taken up by most pharmacy graduates. In the domain of sales, numerous job opportunities are available. These have excellent salary structure. You can also get lucrative incentives that are offered upon good performance. This job is challenging, but higher salary, recognition, perks and promotions are some of the excellent benefits.

LECTURER:

First Class students are eligible to teach as lecturers in the D Pharm programme in D.Pharm Colleges after 03 Years of experience in pharma sector.

PHARMACY BUSINESS:

After going through all of the ethical and legal requirements set forth by the monitoring agencies, the main employment responsibility will be to distribute and dispense medicines in either retail or wholesale, regionally or across India, while ensuring that safe and correct medicines are delivered to the public.
