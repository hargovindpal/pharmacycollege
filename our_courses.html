OUR COURSES

B.Pharmacy:

Accredited by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi and affiliated to Dr. A.P.J. AKTU, Lucknow, Bachelor of Pharmacy is a four year undergraduate course well formulated to support students with the traits and analysis of pharmaceuticals. The course expertise the students regarding medicines, their usage and inculcates relevant technical skills.

The Pharmacy Council of India, the foremost authority to regulate Pharmacy profession in India, decides the pedagogy for the programme. The core pharmacy subjects includes Pharmaceutical Chemistry, Pharmacology, Pharmacognosy and Pharmaceutics. The course mainly focuses on making the students industry-ready.

B. Pharm also initiates a platform of real exposure for the students through practical examinations and industrial visits. The programme emphasizes preparing the graduates with excellent technical efficiency and effective fundamentals. The students have the privilege to experience the best guidance on the subject under the aegis of competent faculty that is ever ready to solve even the most challenging issues related to Pharmaceutical Sciences.

The course is dedicated to individuals who aspire to make some difference in the medical field through their active support and cooperation. It also promotes the skills of multitasking and communication among the students.The programme inspires the students to extend their active contribution to the healthcare system and use the applications of Pharmaceutical Sciences for society.

B.Pharmacy (Lateral Entry):

Accredited by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi and affiliated to Dr. A.P.J. AKTU, Lucknow, B. Pharm Lateral Entry is an option for students who have completed a diploma in Pharmacy to directly enter the second year of a Bachelor's degree in Pharmacy. This pathway provides an opportunity for diploma holders to continue their education and pursue a higher degree.

The Lateral Entry program typically takes three years to complete, as opposed to the regular four-year Bachelor's degree program. This is because students entering the program have already completed the first year of the Bachelor's degree curriculum through their diploma program. As a result, they are exempted from the first-year courses and can directly join the second year.

To be eligible for B. Pharm Lateral Entry, students must have completed a diploma in Pharmacy from a recognized institution. They must also meet the minimum eligibility criteria for admission, which may vary depending on the institution.

Overall, B. Pharm Lateral Entry is an excellent opportunity for diploma holders to continue their education and pursue a higher degree in Pharmacy. The program provides them with advanced knowledge and practical training, which can help them excel in their careers and make a positive impact in the field.

D. Pharmacy:

Accredited by the Pharmacy Council of India, New Delhi and affiliated to B.T.E, Lucknow, Diploma in Pharmacy is a two-year academic course well designed to render thorough knowledge of medicines, procedure involved in their composition and pharmacy management. The course basically renders a layout of principles pertaining to the science of pharmacy that contains the subjects like Pharmaceutical Chemistry, Pharmacology, and Pharmaceutics. Apart from this, the programme also throws light on all types of drugs used for various diseases, their dosage, action and reaction mechanism, adverse effects and much more.

The Pharmacy Council of India, the regulatory authority responsible for the pharmacy profession in India, prescribes the curriculum for D.Pharm.

D. Pharm supports the students to absorb multiple ways of effective medication and use their sharp research skills in industrial world. The programme opens up the doors for various career choices in the field of medicine. By the end of the programme, students may find the jobs of Quality Analyst, Production Executive, Pharmacist, Medical Transcriptionist and Scientific Officer in their kitty. These profiles refer to the reputable career that is indeed fulfilling for the aspirants. Those looking forward for advanced studies can go ahead for higher education.
